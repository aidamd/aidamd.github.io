About

I am Aida (to pronounce it, just say "eye" + "duh!"), a fourth-year computer science PhD student at University of Southern California (USC) and a member of Computational Social Science Lab (CSSL) working with Morteza Dehghani.

My main research interests include designing and developing Natural Language Processing models to answer social science questions. In several projects, I have applied NLP models for identifying moral rhetoric in social media posts. Moreover, I conduct interdisciplinary researcher for understanding and fair detection of hate speech, and it spreads through social media platforms. More recently, my focus has been on the effects of human biases and stereotypes on the design and implementation of supervised machine learning models.

Feel free to Email me, check my Resume, explore my Google Scholar, clone my projects on GitHub, or even connect via LinkedIn!
