About

I recieved my Ph.D. in Computer Science from University of Southern California (USC) as a member of the Computational Social Science Lab (CSSL) supervised by Morteza Dehghani.

My main interests include Natural Language Processing, Fairness and Ethics of AI, and Computational Social Science. I am specifically interested in understanding the effects of social biases on developing unfair machine learning models. My recent research focuses on the impact of cognitive biases and stereotypes on the design and implementation of supervised NLP models. My previous research has led to developing NLP models to identify moral expressions in language, detect unreported hate crime incidents from local news articles, and mitigate bias in hate speech classification.
